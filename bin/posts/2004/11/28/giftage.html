Giftage

I’ve been on the Interweb for A Long Goddamn Time, relatively speaking. Wishlists have been around for not so much of that Long Goddamn Time, but long enough that you’d think I would’ve used them at some point. Not the case.



Yesterday Warren linked to The Doctor Pepper Show, which instantly floored me. It’s drawn by a teenage anime geek girl, the kind you’d expect to have an LJ, and does, and by extension the kind you’d expect to have an Amazon wishlist, but didn’t.



So I emailed her. I said “I’ve never ever been moved to give someone a random something for making good content, but your content makes me want to do this thing.” And she made a wishlist. And I bought her this book I have and enjoy and can vouch for. It was a simple, satisfying social transaction.



Her comic is brilliant. It does things with the comic book/manga format I’ve never seen, and some things I had seen but not seen enough of. It’s messed up in the best way. It makes me eyes happy. It brought me to patronage. That is what good content does.
