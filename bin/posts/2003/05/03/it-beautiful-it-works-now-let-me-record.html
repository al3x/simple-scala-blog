It’s Beautiful, It Works, Now Let Me Record!

---
-

My new 30GB iPod is, in a word, sextastic. I’ll also accept “supercalafragilistic.” Not only is it, from the packaging to the device itself, a total object (read in dripping French accent), but its ease of use is unparalleled. There was no geek-out time necessary. That is to say, the time I had reserved to “figure out how to get it working” was utterly unnecessary. Plugged it into my iBook, it found iTunes, synced and charged, and a couple hours later I was blasting Venetian Snares down I-95 (via a little Belkin FM adaptor, which, for the record, is passable but far from elegant). I was almost dumbfounded, particularly after years of fighting to get anything at all to interface with Linux. It’s just so utterly elegant.



What’s more, it turns Apple did add line-in recording capability to the new iPods, and they may be releasing an adaptor to enable this feature in the near future. This rocked my world. I’ve wanted, for years, a portable digital recording device that doesn’t suck. As an (amateur) electronic musician, being able to record any interesting sound I come across would be a total godsend. It’s the audio equivalent of always having a camera on you. If Apple (or a third party manufacturer) can really make this happen, I’ll plotz. And then I’ll give them my money.
