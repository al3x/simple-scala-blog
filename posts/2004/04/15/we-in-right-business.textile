h1. We’re In the Right Business
Business 2.0 on the "coming high-tech arms race in political campaigns":http://www.business2.com/b2/web/articles/print/0,17925,598894,00.html.
One point I take umbrage with is the proclamation by a consultant that “CRM tools are moving into campaigns. That’s what this is all about!”  This was certainly a point of note at PICnet’s last roundtable on politics online, but several smart folks at the table were uneasy about the lumbering technological giants that are %(=caps)CRM% systems being used for campaigns.  Such bloated, high-cost, difficult-to-use systems are "money pits":http://www.computerweekly.com/Article105058.htm at Fortune 500s with multi-year implementation strategies; campaigns may be increasingly run like businesses, but that’s no reason they should pick up bad business strategies.  Instead, the benefits of full-blown CRMs should be matched with alternative strategies tailored to the needs of a campaigns.  
I’m seeing articles of this sort almost daily.  Makes me think I’m in the right business.
